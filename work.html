The Problem

Most recipe apps that are available have a lot of features that make them convenient and easy to use but they lack efficiency in offering an appropriate recipe for the user who hardly has time to look into cooking something new.

The Solution

A responsive web app that offer two key functionalities. The feature will help users plan a meal based on the ingredients the user has. Users will no longer feel frustrated with thinking about what to cook or how long it will take because they will be using familiar ingredients.

User Flows

I wanted the experience to be as fluid as possible without any friction to the user. I incorporated an advanced search function which allows users to further refine their search amongst a plethora of recipes. Users are also able to customize their preference through the Dietary Needs page and Virtual Fridge feature.

Low-Fideity Prototypes

Initial ideas are quickly sketched on paper to be refined and developed further. The goal, at this stage, is to note down all ideas that comes to mind rather than perfecting the look of each screen. I kept in mind of the special features such as Virtual Fridge and Grocery List.

Mid-Fidelity Prototypes

With the results from the usability testing, the prototype is further refined in Adobe XD. Errors have been corrected and the placement of images and texts are defined in a template. A simple grid layout is used to establish visual heirarchy.
